## Elasticity between R&D and Sales

### (i) Model with constant elasticity

A model that implies a constant elasticity between rd and sales is:
[
\log(rd) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \log(sales) + u
]

In this model, **β₁ is the elasticity**, as it measures the percentage change in rd for a 1% change in sales.

---

### (ii) Estimated model and interpretation

From the estimation, we get:
[
\widehat{\log(rd)} = -4.10 + 1.076 \log(sales)
]

The coefficient on log(sales) is **1.076**, which implies that a **1% increase in sales is associated with approximately a 1.076% increase in rd**, holding other factors constant.

This suggests that rd is **elastic with respect to sales**, meaning that rd increases slightly more than proportionally as sales increase.

The intercept is **-4.10**, which corresponds to log(sales) = 0 (i.e. sales = 1). While this has a mathematical interpretation, it does not have much economic meaning in practice.

The (R^2) is **0.91**, meaning that about **91% of the variation in rd is explained by sales**, which indicates a very strong fit of the model.

The p-value is extremely small, meaning the coefficient on log(sales) is **highly statistically significant**, and we reject the null hypothesis that sales has no effect on rd.

---

### Final note

Overall, the model suggests a strong and statistically significant relationship between sales and R&D expenditure, with rd responding more than proportionally to changes in sales.
